# Prof. Dr. Julian Schröter

I am Professor of Digital Literary Studies at LMU Munich. My research connects computational literary studies, literary theory, and the history of nineteenth-century German literature. I am particularly interested in how methods from machine learning, corpus analysis, and large language models can contribute to literary-historical questions without losing sight of conceptual ambiguity, interpretive practice, and genre theory.

My current work focuses on the methodology of computational literary studies, the history and theory of the German novella and journal prose in the nineteenth century, and quantitative approaches to literary history, canonization, and genre.

## Research interests

- Methodology of computational literary studies, including machine learning and large language models
- Novella and media history of German nineteenth-century literature
- Literary and genre theory, including the modeling of literary history
