# About

I am Professor of Digital Literary Studies at LMU Munich. My research lies at the intersection of computational literary studies, German literary history, literary theory, and machine learning. I work on methods for modeling literary-historical phenomena, especially genre, canonization, and interpretive categories that are conceptually flexible or historically unstable.

A major strand of my work concerns the German novella and nineteenth-century journal prose. My habilitation project develops a digital history of novellas and journal prose in the nineteenth century and asks how computational methods can help model the historical dynamics of genre.

## Research interests

- Methodology of computational literary studies, including machine learning and large language models
- Novella and media history of German nineteenth-century literature
- Literary and genre theory, including the modeling of literary history
